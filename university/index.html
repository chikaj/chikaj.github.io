Research foci

I focus on GISience projects that integrate human-environment interaction. I do a few other things, too.

Remote Sensing

I do satellite image stuff!

GIScience

I do database and spatial analysis stuff!

Open Source

I use as many open source tools as I can! That means QGIS, of course, but also Python-based tools like rasterio, scipy and TensorFlow. And for development, Docker with Django for the backend and Svelte for the frontend.

The Currit Lab

I coordinate much of my work with students in The Currit Lab.

Teaching philosophy

I enjoy teaching and learning...and I do both in my classes. I teach students and they often teach me. The collaborative classrooms I strive to create enhance the learning experiences for everyone involved.

Regularly taught University courses

  • Geographic Applications of Remote Sensing (Graduate)

    The is an accelerated course.

  • Exploring Spatial Databases (Graduate)

    This course begins with ontologies and geospatial semantics, advances to Entity-Relationship Diagrams, and concludes with basic to advanced SQL. PostgreSQL with the PostGIS geospatial extention is used in the course.

  • Remote Sensing and Earth Observation (Undergraduate)

    This course is an introduction to the principles and practices of orbital and sub-orbital Earth Observation.

  • Advanced Geographic Information Systems (Undergraduate)

    This course covers advanced raster and vector analyses in a GIS environment.
